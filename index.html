About Me

My name is Mamadou D’Elhadj Bah. I grew up between the United States and Guinea, West Africa, an experience that shaped how I see people, community, and opportunity. I want the people around me to feel comfortable, safe, and open — whether we’re having a conversation or working on something together.

My interest in technology started early. Growing up with a computer at home sparked my curiosity and eventually turned into a real passion for building and creating with technology. I enjoy learning new things and pushing myself to grow, both personally and professionally.

When I care about a project, I’m quiet but fully focused. I take my time to do things right and give my full effort, because finishing without regret matters to me. When challenges come up, I reflect, learn from mistakes, ask for feedback, and remind myself that growth takes patience. .

Beyond my own goals, I care deeply about impact. I hope to use what I learn to give back — especially through education and sports — and help create opportunities for future generations, particularly in Guinea. At the core of everything I do is a simple belief: be good to others, keep working hard, and trust the process. .

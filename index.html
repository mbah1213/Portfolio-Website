ABOUT ME

I am Mamadou D'Elhadj Bah, named after my paternal grandfather. I’m currently a second-year student at Rochester Institute of Technology majoring in Web and mobile computing with a minor in Management Information systems. I was born in Maryland (Baltimore) and grew up in a small country in West Africa called Guinea. Apart from speaking English, I am fluent in French and my native language, “Pular.”

I visited a couple of countries with my family: France, Belgium, Netherlands, Germany , Canada and Senegal. I learned to play soccer in the street of my neighborhood with my childhood friends. I was surrounded by a community that loves watching sports. Gradually I started to be passionate about it.

Since I was 5 years old, I have been around computers. I can give the credit to my father, who bought my siblings and me a computer that I could play on and browse computer games for free. Then I got my first video game on PS3, where I played so many fun games with my friends such as FIFA 16 and Need for Speed.

My greatest strength is my ability to learn quickly and adapt to new concepts with ease. However, I sometimes struggle with procrastination when I am confident in my understanding of a task. I have recognized that this approach is not always effective, and I am actively working on improving my time management and productivity.

I aim to become a more independent person and make new friends with similar interests. I am hard-working, responsible, ambitious, enthusiastic, and friendly and I enjoy helping people.
